This online edition presents the **ICF Laws of Carrom** in readable chapters. Each chapter keeps the official wording; **Beginner's guide** callouts explain how those laws work in everyday play.

## New to carrom?

Carrom is finger billiards on a square wooden board. Two players (singles) or four (doubles) use a **striker** to pocket **carrommen** — nine white, nine black, and one red **Queen** — into corner pockets. You sit opposite your opponent, shoot only from your base rectangle, and take turns until one player clears all of their colour.

Under ICF rules, colours are fixed before the break (the breaker plays white). In casual games, the first piece pocketed often decides your colour — see Chapter 4. Tournament scoring is **25 points per game**, best of three games — not the informal 29-point house rules sometimes used at home.
