*Every board begins with the Break — the opening shot that scatters the cluster of 19 coins. This chapter covers how the coins are arranged, who takes the Break, what counts as a valid Break, and how turns alternate through the rest of the board, the game, and the match.*

<div class="callout">
  <div class="callout-title">Beginner's guide — setting up the break</div>
  <p>Place the <strong>Queen in the centre circle</strong>. Arrange the other 18 coins in two compact rings around her, <strong>alternating black and white</strong>, all touching each other inside the outer circle. ICF Law also specifies a <strong>"Y" of three white coins</strong> in the second row — see Rule 41.</p>
  <p>The player who breaks plays <strong>white</strong> for that board; the opponent has black. After the umpire calls <em>Play</em>, the breaker has <strong>15 seconds</strong> to strike. In casual play, a coin toss decides who breaks first; turns continue as long as you pocket your own coins.</p>
</div>

## Arranging the Coins for the Break

**41.** &nbsp;&nbsp;a) Before the break, the C/m are so arranged, in a flat position, that the **Queen shall occupy the Centre Circle** and the rest of the C/m are placed around the Queen in the first row, keeping the Black and White C/m alternately.

In the second row, three white C/m form the shape **"Y"**, with the white C/m in the first row. The remaining space is filled by placing black and white C/m alternately. All c/m so arranged should be in compact round, touching each other, within the Outer Circle. A player may use his fingers or striker to keep the C/m intact.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b) The C/m should be arranged for the break with least possible loss of time after each board.

## Who Breaks

**42.** The Break is taken by the player who has chosen to strike first.

**43.** The player who is to break shall have the **white C/m** during that board, leaving the black C/m to his opponent. The Queen shall be the common C/m.

## What Counts as a Break

**44.** Break is considered to have been made if the Striker touches any of the C/m, even slightly.

**45.** &nbsp;&nbsp;a) Break is **not** considered made if no C/m is touched by the Striker in its usual run or jumping out. A maximum of **two more chances** shall be allowed.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b) If after the permissible number of chances no C/m is touched, the right to Break shall be lost. The turn passes on to the opponent, who will have black C/m for play. No re-arrangement of the C/m shall be permitted. This condition prevails until the break is affected.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c) If a player in his attempt to break plays an improper stroke or pockets his striker without touching any of the c/m, he loses his turn. However, a Due or penalty shall **not** be applicable.

## When the Break Begins

**46.** The break shall be taken only after the Umpire calls **Play**, and the stroke shall be made within **15 seconds** of such call. The play is deemed to have begun from the moment the Umpire calls *Play*.

<div class="callout">
  <div class="callout-title">Don't jump the gun</div>
  <p>If the break is made <em>before</em> the Umpire calls Play, all C/m and/or Queen pocketed shall be taken out for placing and a foul declared. The player loses his turn.</p>
</div>

## Turn of Play

**48.** As long as a player pockets his own C/m and/or Queen in accordance with the Laws, his turn shall continue. Otherwise it passes on to the opponent.

**49.** &nbsp;&nbsp;a) The order of Breaking across the games:

  - **First game** — the player who chose to break the first board shall have the white C/m. The turn to break passes alternately during the game.
  - **Second game** — the player who did not have the first turn to break in the first game shall have his turn first.
  - **Third game** — the turn to break passes on to the first player.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b) In doubles, the turn passes on to the player sitting to the **right hand side** of the player who had his turn.

**50.** A player shall not take more than **15 seconds** for making a stroke. The timer starts the moment the C/m, Queen or Striker has come to rest and the striker is picked up by the opponent, or after observing the time limit for placing C/m and/or foregoing due or penalty C/m, if any.

**51.** If the turn is availed by a player who plays **out of turn**, before the Umpire could control, he loses the board by the number of C/m and Queen as were on the C/B at the time of the first out-of-turn stroke. If the above remains unnoticed by the Umpire or opponent till the next stroke is taken, the turn shall be allowed and the next turn shall be as per the Laws.
